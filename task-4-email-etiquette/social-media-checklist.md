# Social Media Responsibility Checklist for College Students

## ✔️ Best Practices
- Build a positive and professional image online.
- Interact respectfully and value different viewpoints.
- Protect your accounts using strong passwords and enable two-factor authentication.
- Post informative, genuine, and meaningful content.
- Use social media in moderation and manage your time effectively.

## ❌ Things to Avoid
- Avoid sharing content that could damage your image or career opportunities.
- Do not reveal sensitive personal information such as your address, phone number, or passwords.
- Do not trust or share information without verifying its authenticity.
- Avoid arguments, trolling, or any form of cyberbullying.
- Do not neglect your mental health by overusing social media.

---

## 📋 Quick Checklist

| Best Practices | Things to Avoid |
|----------------|----------------|
| Communicate respectfully | Share offensive or harmful posts |
| Keep accounts secure | Reveal private information |
| Check facts before sharing | Spread misinformation |
| Manage screen time | Waste excessive time online |
| Stay constructive and positive | Engage in negativity |

---

## 💡 Key Point
Using social media responsibly helps create a strong digital reputation while also safeguarding your personal and future professional life.
